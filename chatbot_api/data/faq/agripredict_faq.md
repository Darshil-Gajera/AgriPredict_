# AgriPredict FAQ — RAG Knowledge Base

## Merit Calculation

**Q: How is the merit score calculated?**
Merit = (Theory Marks / Theory Total) × 200 + (GUJCET Marks / 120) × 100.
Maximum merit is 300. Farming background bonus adds 5% on top of the final merit.

**Q: What theory total options are there?**
Theory total can be Out of 300, Out of 240, or Out of 210 depending on your board.
Gujarat board students typically have 300. Some other boards may have 210 or 240.

**Q: What is the farming background bonus?**
Students whose parents hold a Khedut Khata (farming certificate from Talati office) get +5% bonus.
Example: if your merit is 240, with farming bonus it becomes 252. Maximum is capped at 300.

## Categories & Courses

**Q: What is Category 1?**
Category 1 is for B-Group (PCB) students only.
Courses: B.Sc. (Hons.) Agriculture, B.Sc. Horticulture, B.Sc. Forestry, B.Sc. Biotechnology.

**Q: What is Category 2?**
Category 2 is for A-Group (PCM) and B-Group (PCB) students.
Courses: B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering, B.Tech. Food Technology, B.Tech. Agricultural IT.
For PCM: merit = (Physics+Chemistry+Maths / total) × 200 + GUJCET × 100/120.
For PCB: merit = (Physics+Chemistry+Biology / total) × 200 + GUJCET × 100/120.

**Q: What is Category 3?**
Category 3 is for A-Group and B-Group students.
Courses: B.Sc. Community Science, B.Sc. Food Nutrition & Dietetics.

## Admission Process

**Q: What is the step-by-step admission process?**
1. Fill the online application form at the official Gujarat agriculture admission portal.
2. Upload required documents: Class 10 marksheet, Class 12 marksheet, GUJCET scorecard, category certificate, domicile certificate, farming certificate (if applicable), passport photo, signature.
3. Merit lists are published based on Class 12 theory marks + GUJCET.
4. College allotment happens over 3 rounds + Mop-up round.
5. Accept allotment and report to college.
6. Pay tuition and hostel fees to confirm admission.

**Q: What documents are needed?**
Required: Class 10 marksheet, Class 12 marksheet, GUJCET scorecard, category/caste certificate,
domicile certificate, farming background certificate (Khedut Khata, if applicable), passport photo, signature.

**Q: How many admission rounds are there?**
Typically 3 main rounds (Round 1, Round 2, Round 3) plus a Mop-up round.
Each round has its own merit list and allotment. You must accept/reject within the deadline.

## Scholarships

**Q: What scholarships are available for agriculture students in Gujarat?**
Major scholarships:
1. MYSY (Mukhyamantri Yuva Swavalamban Yojana) — ₹50,000/year, 80%+ in 12th, income ≤ ₹6 lakh
2. NSP (National Scholarship Portal) — SC/ST/OBC/EWS/Minority, ₹10,000–₹30,000
3. ICAR NTS — ICAR seat holders only, ₹24,000/year
4. Digital Gujarat Scholarship — SC/ST/OBC/SEBC/EWS, ₹5,000–₹20,000
5. INSPIRE (DST) — Top 1% in 12th Science, ₹80,000/year for 5 years
6. ONGC Scholarship — SC/ST/EWS, 60%+, income ≤ ₹2 lakh, ₹48,000/year
7. Kanya Kelavani Yojana — Girls only, ₹5,000–₹25,000
8. Sitaram Jindal Foundation — income ≤ ₹2.5 lakh, ₹2,000–₹2,500/month
9. LIC Golden Jubilee — 60%+ in 12th, income ≤ ₹2 lakh, ₹10,000–₹20,000/year
10. Minority Scholarships — 50%+, income ≤ ₹2 lakh, ₹5,000–₹25,000

**Q: How do I apply for MYSY scholarship?**
Visit mysy.guj.nic.in. You need Gujarat domicile, 80%+ in Class 12 (Science stream),
and family income ≤ ₹6 lakh per year. Apply after getting admission confirmation.

**Q: Am I eligible for INSPIRE if I got 90% in 12th?**
INSPIRE requires top 1% of Class 12 Science stream board result. 90% may or may not qualify
depending on your board's rank. Check with online-inspire.gov.in for eligibility verification.

## GUJCET

**Q: What is GUJCET?**
GUJCET (Gujarat Common Entrance Test) is conducted by GSEB (Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board).
For agriculture admissions, the exam covers Biology and Chemistry (for B-Group) or
Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (for A-Group). It is out of 120 marks.

**Q: What happens if I did not appear in GUJCET?**
GUJCET marks are mandatory for computing the merit score for Gujarat agriculture admissions.
Without GUJCET, you cannot participate in the regular merit-based admission process.
